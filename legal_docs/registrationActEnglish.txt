Registration Act, 1908

Part 1 - Preliminary

1. Short title, extent and commencement –
(1) This Act may be called the Registration Act, 1908.
(2) It extends to the whole of India except the State of Jammu and Kashmir.
(3) It shall come into force on the first day of January, 1908.

Part 3 - Of Registrable Documents

17. Documents of which registration is compulsory (Mandatory) -
The following documents must be registered:
(a) Instruments of gift of immovable property (land, house).
(b) Other non-testamentary instruments which create or extinguish any right in immovable property of the value of one hundred rupees or more.
(c) Leases of immovable property for any term exceeding one year.

18. Documents of which registration is optional (Choice) -
The registration of the following documents is not mandatory, it is optional:
(a) Wills.
(b) Instruments relating to immovable property of a value less than one hundred rupees.
(c) Leases of immovable property for a term not exceeding one year.
