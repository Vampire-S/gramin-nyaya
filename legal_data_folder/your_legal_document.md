# Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

## Objective

1. With a view to providing assured income support to small and marginal farmer families in the country, the Government of India has decided to launch a Central Sector Scheme named **Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)** with 100% financial assistance from the Central Government from this financial year.

2. The scheme will provide supplementary income support to small and marginal farmer families for meeting their agricultural inputs and other needs. This will help ensure that expenditure required before the receipt of expected income, particularly after the crop cycle, can be met.

3. The scheme will help farmers meet such expenses and protect them from falling into the clutches of moneylenders. It will ensure continuity in farming activities, enable modernization of agricultural practices, and pave the way for a dignified livelihood.

## Effective Date of the Scheme

4. The scheme shall be implemented with effect from **01.12.2018**, and eligible farmer families shall be entitled to receive benefits for the period commencing from this date.

5. The cut-off date for identification of eligible farmer families is **01.02.2019**. Land ownership as recorded on this date shall be the basis for determining eligibility for assistance.

6. In the event of the death of an eligible farmer after **01.02.2019**, his/her heirs shall also be entitled to receive benefits under the scheme, provided the family falls within the small and marginal farmer category.

7. Any change in the cut-off date for identification of eligible farmer families shall be made only with the approval of the Cabinet.

## Definition of Family

8. An eligible small and marginal farmer family shall consist of **husband, wife, and minor children (below 18 years of age)**, who collectively own cultivable land up to **2 hectares**, as per land records of the States and Union Territories.

## Identification of Eligible Families

9. Based on the Agricultural Census 2015–16, the number of small and marginal farmer families for the year 2018–19 was estimated. Accordingly, the estimated number of operational landholdings belonging to small and marginal farmers during 2018–19 was **13.15 crore**.

10. After excluding families belonging to higher-income categories, the estimated number of eligible beneficiary families is **12.50 crore**.

## Financial Requirement

11. The scheme shall be implemented as a **100% Centrally Funded Scheme**.

12. The estimated expenditure is approximately **₹25,000 crore per four-month installment period** and **₹75,000 crore annually**.

13. A provision of **₹20,000 crore** was proposed through supplementary demands for the year **2018–19**. Similarly, **₹75,000 crore** was proposed for the year **2019–20**.

## Assistance to Eligible Small and Marginal Farmer Families

14. Eligible small and marginal farmer families shall receive **₹6,000 per year**, transferred directly into Aadhaar-linked bank accounts in **three equal installments every four months**.

15. The first installment for the period from **01.12.2018 to 31.03.2019** shall be transferred to eligible families immediately after their identification during the same financial year.

16. Aadhaar shall be mandatory for eligible beneficiaries. From the year **2019–20 onwards**, benefits shall be transferred directly into bank accounts through an Aadhaar-based database.

17. However, for the release of the first installment during **2018–19**, Aadhaar shall be required only for those beneficiaries for whom it is already available. For the remaining beneficiaries, valid identification documents shall be obtained for establishing identity. Aadhaar enrollment shall, however, be mandatory so that future installments can be transferred through the Aadhaar-based database.

18. States and Union Territories, which are responsible for identifying eligible beneficiaries, shall ensure that no ineligible family is selected and that no individual or family receives the benefit more than once.

## Monitoring of Scheme Implementation

19. A **Project Management Unit (PMU)** shall be established under the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare for monitoring the scheme.

20. The Unit shall function under a **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**, who shall be responsible for both implementation of the scheme and its wide publicity and outreach.

21. Similar monitoring arrangements shall be established at the State and District levels. At the Central level, a monitoring committee headed by the Cabinet Secretary has been constituted.
